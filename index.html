About Me

I'm currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Massachusetts General Hospital Institute for Technology Assessment at the Harvard Medical School. In August 2021, I will join the H. Milton Stewart School of Industrial and Systems Engineering at Georgia Tech as an Assistant Professor. I obtained my PhD in 2020 and M.S. in 2016 from the Department of Industrial and Operations Engineering at the University of Michigan and my B.S. in Industrial Engineering from the University of Pittsburgh in 2014.

In general, I am interested decision-making under uncertainty. My current research aims to develop data-driven frameworks for Predictive Analytics and Prescriptive Analytics as motivated by high-impact problems in Medical Decision-Making for the areas of concussion, glaucoma, and cardiovascular disease. This field draws many problems which have both practical impact and interesting theoretical challenges.

Over the past few years, I have served in various leadership roles for INFORMS at UM. In my service efforts, I am especially interested in increasing diversity in STEM through mentorship and outreach.
